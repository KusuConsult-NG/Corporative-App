# ANCHORAGE WELFARE SAVINGS AND LOANS MULTIPURPOSE COOPERATIVE SOCIETY LIMITED 

## BYE-LAW

---

## PART ONE (1) - GENERAL PROVISIONS

### ARTICLE 1: NAME AND ADDRESS

It shall be known and called **ANCHORAGE WELFARE SAVINGS AND LOANS MULTIPURPOSE COOPERATIVE SOCIETY LIMITED**.

### ARTICLE 2: REGISTERED OFFICE

The registered office of the Anchorage Welfare Savings and Loans Multipurpose Cooperative Society Limited shall be **University of Jos Library, Jos, Plateau State Nigeria**.

### ARTICLE 3: EVIDENCE OF REGISTRATION

Anchorage Welfare Savings and Loans Multipurpose Cooperative Society Limited (AWSLMCSL) is registered with the Plateau State Ministry of Commerce and Industry.

**Registration Number**: PL 26267

## PART TWO (2) - POWER OF THE BYE-LAWS

### ARTICLE 4: BINDEDNESS OF THE BYE-LAWS

(a) The provisions of the bye-law shall be binding on all those who subscribe to the membership of the cooperative Society.

(b) Any Bye-law, regulation, rule or instruction of the Cooperative that is in force, that is inconsistent with the Nigerian Cooperative Societies Act Cap. ‚Ä¶ Laws of Federation of Nigeria, (2004) shall to the extent of the inconsistency, be void.

(c) These Bye-Laws enjoins all members to be of equal stakeholders in the success of the Cooperative Society. The Cooperative members shall therefore:
   - i. Have equal opportunities in the affairs of the Cooperative Society
   - ii. Accord each other, mutual respect and understanding

---

## PART THREE (3) - AREAS OF OPERATION AND OBJECTIVES

### ARTICLE 6: AREAS OF OPERATION

The area of operation of the society shall be in Jos and its environs. However, it shall be extended to other parts of Nigeria where existing members of the Cooperative Society relocate to.

### ARTICLE 7: OBJECTIVES

The main objective of the Society is to promote the economic interest of its members and to:

(i) To promote and develop cooperative ideas and improve members' economic conditions by means of common business ventures.

(ii) To encourage thrift and provide a means where savings in the society could be made regularly.

(iii) To encourage, help and create opportunities for members to develop themselves financially.

(iv) To sensitize and create awareness among members on investment opportunities.

(v) To engage in any legitimate business the Executive Commitment deems beneficial to the Society.

(vi) To invest the society's money in online based investment opportunities so as to make profit for its members, which shall enable members to earn bonus as may be decided by the Executive Committee.

(vii) To carry out any such or other objectives incidental or connected to the above and which is within the Cooperative guidelines.

### ARTICLE 9: GENERAL MEMBERSHIP

(i) Membership of the AWSLMCSL is open to staff of the University of Jos residing within the area of operations.

(ii) Only persons that are eighteen (18) years and above shall qualify to be members.

(iii) Application for membership shall be made on a prescribed form of the Cooperative Society.

(iv) The executive committee shall screen application forms and admits members as appropriate.

(v) Any member on joining the society shall pay a registration fees as shall be determined by Executive Committee.

### ARTICLE 10: CORPORATE MEMBERSHIP

i. Corporate bodies that desire to be members shall be granted the status of membership as individual persons who subscribe to the membership of the society as enshrined by these Bye-laws. However, such corporate bodies must attach a photocopy of the certificate of their registration with the Corporate Affairs Commission to the prescribed application form for membership.

ii. Cooperative Societies can also apply to as secondary members as stipulated under the Nigerian Cooperative Act, 2004.

### ARTICLE 11: TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP

Membership shall terminate by:
(a) Death
(b) Permanent insanity
(c) Expulsion by two thirds majority at a General meeting
(d) Voluntary withdrawal

### ARTICLE 12: WITHDRAWAL OF MEMBERSHIP

i. A member is free to withdraw his membership anytime he wishes EXCEPT that such withdrawal shall be with the approval of the Executive Committee on terms stated hereunder.

ii. Members who are indebted to the Society shall not withdraw their membership until they liquidate all their indebtedness.

iii. On withdrawal, the member shall be paid all his entitlements less his indebtedness to the Society.

iv. A processing fee of Two Thousand Naira (‚Ç¶2,000.00) only shall be deducted from his entitlement as administrative charges.

### ARTICLE 13: EXPULSION OF MEMBERS

A member shall be expelled on the following ground:

(a) Conviction for any criminal offence by a competent Court of Law
(b) Any action which is considered by the executive committee to be dishonest or contrary to the collective interest of member or stated objectives of the society
(c) An expelled member shall not be entitled to any interest on his accumulated savings

## PART FIVE (5) - REVENUE GENERATION AND MANAGEMENT

### ARTICLE 18: SAVINGS

a) There shall be minimum monthly saving of One Thousand Naira (‚Ç¶1,000.00) subject to review by the congress

b) Members are free to review their savings at any time on a prescribed form but not less than the minimum monthly savings.

c) Interest of up to five percent (5%) per annum may be paid on members' block savings, subject to the overall financial performance, liquidity position, and surplus of the Cooperative, as determined and approved by the Executive Committee.

d) On withdrawal of membership, the sum of One Thousand Naira (‚Ç¶100.00) only (subject to review by the congress) shall be deducted as process fee.

e) Withdrawal of savings is not allowed.

### ARTICLE 19: FIXED DEPOSIT

A member may make fixed cash deposit at an interest rate of 10% per annum for a period not less than (12 months) subject to an approval by the Executive Committee. Withdrawal made before the period shall attract no interest.

### ARTICLE 20: USE OF SOCIETY FUNDS

The society's funds shall be devoted to the promotion of the set objectives and other purposes set forth in these bye-laws.

### ARTICLE 21: INVESTMENTS

(i) All investments decisions shall be made by the Executive Committee subject to approval by the board of trustees (Board of Trustees).

(ii) The society's investments shall be in the following areas:
   a. Purchase and sales of essential commodities
   b. Treasury bills
   c. Purchase of shares in viable companies
   d. Fixed deposits and other investments in reputable banks
   e. In movable and immovable properties
   f. Leasing of property and other viable investment considered by the Executive Committee

---

## PART SIX (6) - MEETINGS

### ARTICLE 22: ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING (AGM)

i. General meeting to be known as ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING (AGM) shall be held in the first quarter of the financial year after the external auditors shall have audited the society's accounts.

ii. General Meeting (GM) shall be held twice (2) within the financial year.

iii. The presence of at least two-third of the Members of the Board of Trustees shall be necessary for the disposal of any business of the day.

iv. Notices of meetings shall be given fourteen (14) days before the date of such meeting through either email addresses or any social media platform that a member provides to the administrative office of the Cooperative.

v. All minutes must be made available and presented to members by the Secretary, via the group information system.

### ARTICLE 25: VOTING RIGHTS

(a) Each Founding Member shall be entitled to two (2) votes.
(b) Every member shall be entitled to one vote each.
(c) Voting by proxy is not allowed at any meeting.
(d) No member in arrears of ordinary savings shall be allowed to vote or be voted for.
(e) Where there is a tie, the chairman shall have a casting vote-right.

---

## PART SEVEN (7) - ADMINISTRATIVE STRUCTURE

### ARTICLE 27: BOARD OF TRUSTEES

For the purpose of accountability, checks and balance and adherence to the law, the Board of Trustees (BOT) shall be constituted.

### ARTICLE 31: THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

(a) AWSLMCSL shall have an Executive Committee who shall be saddled with the general administration of the Cooperative Society.

(b) The Executive Committee shall consist of:
   - Chairman
   - Vice Chairman
   - Secretary
   - Assistant Secretary
   - Public Relations Officer
   - Financial Secretary
   - Treasurer
   - Legal Adviser
   - Two (2) founding members

(c) All Executive Officers shall be elected at the Annual General Meeting.

(d) All elected Executive Officers shall hold office for two years. An officer shall be eligible for re-election for another tenure of two (2) years.

---

## PART EIGHT (8) - ANNUAL REPORT

### ARTICLE 33: ANNUAL REPORT

(a) Before each Annual General Meeting (AGM), the Secretary shall compile the annual report for the year in a book form.

(b) The Annual Report shall include reports of the Chairman, Secretary, Businesses Committee, Loans, Accounts and Finance Committee, Internal and External Auditor's reports.

All Annual Reports shall include among others:
   i. Annual Financial Statements of Profit and Loss Accounts
   ii. Balance sheets
   iii. Statement of sources and Application of Funds

---

## PART NINE (9) - RECORDS

### ARTICLE 34: BOOKS AND FORMS

The following books and registers shall be maintained:

(a) A register of members
(b) A register showing member's attendance at meetings
(c) A cashbook showing receipts and payments
(d) A general ledger
(e) A personal ledger containing account of each member
(f) A register showing all necessary information concerning loans
(g) A minutes-book
(h) A register of fixed deposits
(i) Bonds securing loans
(j) A file containing a copy of the Nigerian Cooperative Societies Act

---

## PART TEN (10) - ADMINISTRATION OF FINANCES

### ARTICLE 36: APPROVAL OF RELEASES

i. All payments must be properly approved by the appropriate authority.

ii. Only the Executive Committee members shall approve all investment payments, all loans, and all other payments above Ten Million Naira (‚Ç¶10,000,000.00) only.

iii. The Financial Secretary shall keep custody of all financial records and make sure that they are up to date at all times.

iv. Bank reconciliation shall be prepared monthly.

v. An imprest of Fifty Thousand Naira (‚Ç¶50,000.00) only, shall be kept with the treasurer.

---

## PART ELEVEN (11) - BUDGET AND ALLOCATIONS

### ARTICLE 38: BANKERS OF THE SOCIETY

Bankers of the Society shall be:
i. Zenith Bank
ii. Any other viable bank subject to approval by the Congress

### ARTICLE 39: DISPOSAL OF SURPLUS FUND

The annual net surplus of the society shall be appropriated in the following manner:

i. 20% to General reserve
ii. 10% to EXCO members
iii. 5% to Committee Members
iv. 15% to Board of Trustees Members
v. 50% to members

---

## PART TWELVE (12) - ELECTORAL MATTERS

### ARTICLE 41: ELECTION

There shall be an election before an Annual General Meeting as stipulated under this Bye-laws into all elective post except for members of the Board of Trustees.

i. Election shall be by secret ballot
ii. A member shall be declared a winner by simple majority
iii. There shall be a bye-election to replace an EXCO member who may be away for a period of six (6) calendar months

---

## PART THIRTEEN (13) - RESOLUTION OF DISPUTES

### ARTICLE 45: DISPUTES RESOLUTION

Any disputes concerning this Bye-Laws or the business of the society shall be referred to the Director for settlement by arbitration as stipulated by the Nigerian Cooperative Societies Act, the Society's Bye-laws, and all relevant regulations.

### ARTICLE 46: SANCTIONS FOR BREACHES

(i) Any criminal breach of trust shall be handled according to the Criminal law of Nigeria.

(ii) An Executive Committee can be impeached by two-third majority of the General Congress members in attendance at a meeting for that purpose.

(iii) An Executive Committee member may be impeached on the grounds of fraud, dishonesty and incompetence.

---

## PART FOURTEEN (14) - POWER FOR LIQUIDATION

### ARTICLE 47: LIQUIDATION

(i) The Society shall be liquidated only by the order of the Registrar as stipulated under Sections 44 and 45 of the Nigerian Cooperative Societies Act, (2004).

(ii) In case there is any surplus after meeting all liabilities of the society and repaying all sums at the credit of members, such surplus shall be disposed of to members as interest on their savings.

---

## PART FIFTEEN (15) - AMENDMENTS AND DISSOLUTION

### ARTICLE 48: AMENDMENTS OF BYE-LAWS

This Bye-Law shall be opened to amendments by the Congress at an interval of five years from the date of its commencement.

### ARTICLE 49: DISSOLUTION

The Cooperative may be dissolved at any time if agreed by two third (2/3) of those members present and voting at a general meeting.

---

## INTERPRETATION CLAUSE

### ARTICLE 50: DEFINITIONS

(i) **BOARD OF TRUSTEES**: The highest and final decision-making body of AWSLMCSL.

(ii) **MEMBER**: Any person who has successfully passed through the prescribed conditions for admission and has been formally accepted as a stakeholder.

(iii) **EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE (EXCO)**: The elected governing body of the Society to whom the Management of its affairs is entrusted.

(iv) **FINANCIAL YEAR**: The period of twelve calendar months beginning from the 1st of January and ends on the 31st of December.

(v) **FOUNDING MEMBERS**: Those who initiated the formation and registration of AWSLMCSL.

(vi) **THE ACT**: The Nigerian Cooperative Society Act of 2004.

(vii) **THE REGISTRAR**: The Registrar of Cooperative Societies, Plateau State.

(viii) **SOCIETY**: The Anchorage Welfare Savings and Loans Multipurpose Cooperative Society Limited.
